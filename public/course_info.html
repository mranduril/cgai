Course Information

Overview

This course focuses on emerging AI-powered computer graphics techniques that have enabled the many exciting groundbreaking applications across computer graphics, 3D vision, and generative AI. We will begin with the mathematical foundations and historical context of traditional computer graphics, providing a comprehensive overview of the evolution of these modern techniques throughout the history of graphics. The core topics include neural implicit functions (e.g., SDFs), neural radiance fields (NeRFs), Gaussian splatting, generative graphics, and differentiable physics.

Late-Day Policy

Every student is allotted seven late days for assignments. However, these late days are not applicable to the final project or quizzes. You are free to use these late days at any point during the quarter to accommodate your schedule. To utilize a late day, please specify this in the comment section of your assignment submission so our TAs are informed. Should you wish to waive a late day or require an extension beyond these seven days, please seek permission from Bo.

If your assignment is submitted at times such as 12:04 am or 12:20 am, Canvas may categorize it as a late submission. However, we do not classify this as a late submission. If the assignment is submitted within a reasonable time frame, it will not activate a late day. We always intend not to rush toward deadlines but to focus on creating more perfect graphics.
