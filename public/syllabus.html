Syllabus

Introduction to Computer Graphics in AI Era (Week 1)

  • Objectives: Overview of computer graphics in AI and course structure overview.
  • Lectures: Computer Graphics in AI, Course Overview

Unit I: Implicit Neural Representation (Week 2-4)

  • Objectives: Explore the mathematical and algorithmic foundations for implicit neural representations, focusing on the Signed Distance Field (SDF) as a universal shape representation
  • Lectures: SDF Foundation, SDF Combination, SDF Rendering (Ray Marching), SDF Representation, Neural Field and Deep SDF
  • Assignments: (A) SDF and Ray Marching, and (B) Neural SDF

Unit II: Neural Radiance Field (Week 5-7)

  • Objectives: Explore the mathematical foundations of radiance fields, volume rendering equations, and Neural Radiance Field (NeRF)
  • Lectures: Radiance Field, Rendering Equations, Participating Media and Volumetric Rendering, Neural Radiance Field, Neural Field Data Structures
  • Assignments: (A) Volumetric Rendering and (B) NeRF

Unit III: Gaussian Splatting (Week 8-10)

  • Objectives: Investigate point-based graphics, differentiable rendering, and its modern evolution into 3D Gaussian Splatting
  • Lectures: Points, Splats, Differentiable Surfaces, Meshfree Representation and Rendering, Gaussian Splatting
  • Assignment: Point-based Rendering and Gaussian Splatting

Unit IV: Position-based Physics Simulation (Week 11-13)

  • Objectives: Study physics-based simulation techniques, emphasizing position-based dynamics (PBD) and their applications in AI
  • Lectures: Foundation of Physics Simulation, Position-based Dynamics, Differentiable Physics Simulation
  • Assignment: Position-based Dynamics Simulation

Unit V: Advanced Topics (Week 14-15)

  • Objectives: Explore cutting-edge generative graphics models and their practical applications in modern computer graphics.
  • Lectures: Generative graphics, diffusion model, LLM-based visual content creation
